I am a Master student in Robotics at Delft University of Technology (TU Delft), the Netherlands. Before that, I obtained my Bachelor’s degree in Mechanical Engineering with Excellent Graduate Honor from Southern University of Science and Technology (SUSTech), Shenzhen, China.

Inspired by the fantasies of Jules Verne and Isaac Asimov, I am captivated by the unparalleled elegance of robotics, which propels me to further explore robotic intelligence to promote the widespread applications of intelligent systems.

During my bachelor period, I conducted research on Passive Assisted Exoskeleton and constructed the functional prototype under the supervision of Prof. Hongqiang Wang. In addition, I collaborated closely with Dr. Jianwen Luo from AIRS, focusing on the mechanical design of Quadruped Robots.

Currently, my research interests lie in Robot Planning and Perception, especially the intersection area of robotics navigation and machine learning. During my graduate period, I worked on the motion planning of industrial robots at ROKAE Robotics in summer 2021. Since February 2022, I have been a computer vision engineer at Lely Technologies, focusing on algorithm development for real-time multi-camera image stitching. In July 2020, I started my master thesis about active SLAM supervised by Elia Bonetto and Prof. Aamir Ahmad at the Flight Robotics and Perception Group (FRPG) of Max-Planck Institute for Intelligent Systems, Tübingen.

  2. Patent
    Power-assisted Exoskeleton Device
    Chen, L., Liu, H., and Xu, C., et al.
    ZL 201921545381.6, Aug 2020
    Abs PDF

    The power-assisted exoskeleton device comprises a wearable waistcoat and an arm supporting mechanism. Wherein the two groups of arm supporting mechanisms are symmetrically fixed on a supporting seat of the wearable vest. The arm supporting mechanism comprises a first supporting rod, a second supporting rod, an elastic mechanism and the like. The self-adaption of the supporting force can achieve different magnitudes at the same position. The device has the advantages that an external power source is not needed, the device is simple in structure and convenient to carry, and stable supporting force can be provided during work.

Upper-Limb Passive Adaptive Assisted Exoskeleton
Video Robot Design Exoskeleton
The passive adjustable arm-exoskeleton is designed based on a spring slider model and four-bar-linkage model. It is a lightweight wearable system with a weight of 2 kg and with a feature of easy adjustability. The lifting force of the mechanism is relatively constant in a wide working range and will step down in the non-working area.
Multi-Camera Real-Time Surveillance Video Stitching
Code Computer Vision Image Stitching In Progress
Based on the AutoStitch framework, the feature matching strategy is developed given the corresponding ROIs since the cameras for surveillance are of constant parameters. Furthermore, seam-based optimization will be implemented to improve the stitching performance of the overlapping area.
Online Trajectory Planning for Manipulators based on Discrete-Time Double S Profile
Code Online Planning
Implemented real-time path following movement based on the PID method and double S profile. The constraint-based PID method can achieve synchronous movement for all joints within dynamics constraints. The discrete-time double S method can achieve its maximum kinematic properties for each joint.
